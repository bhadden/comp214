Research and Report on Rapid Interface Design Tools

Amico Sketchify

AMICO Sketchify (AKA AMICO Sketchpad and the Java-based Sketchlet) is a rapid prototyping tool, designed by Željko Obrenović. Sketchify applies the power of computing to the convenience and simplicity of pen-and-paper in order for developers to leverage the ability to rapidly design and explore ideas. It also allows for front-end developers, who may not have a deep understanding of the underlying code to explore the concepts and functionality of a user interface quickly and easily. Sketchify can take input through drawing, sensors, audio etc. and create a quick and simple interactive sketch, which in turn can illustrate how two or more components can interact and/or affect one another. The below example illustrates the UI of an iPod and how it can be interacted with.

This type of low-fidelity prototyping can be quick and simple, but highly effective at allowing creators and developers easily and efficiently bring early ideas to life. This can be useful in providing proof-of-concept to interested parties and can help convey the big-picture or end-goal early on in the development cycle. It not only provides an early chance to visualize, but also helps with solving UI logic and navigation elements.
