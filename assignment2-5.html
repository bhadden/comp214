Describe a Large-Scale Display System

Brook Hadden (3461448)
July 08, 2020

Mercedes-Benz Stadium Halo Board

The Mercedes-Benz Stadium, home to the Atlanta Falcons NFL team, houses one of the largest single display boards in the world. Aptly-named the “Halo”, it is a 58’ x 1100’ ring-shaped display board that sits just below the roof of the stadium and occupies 62,350 square feet worth of real-estate. It was custom-built by the company Daktronics, which makes many large-scale displays for stadiums and arenas around the world. If you were to stretch out the display into a single column, it would be taller than the Eiffel tower.

The screen itself is made up of 616 sections, each of which are made up of smaller panels, containing over 37 million LEDs. Because of the weight, custom, 277V power supplies were created for the panels in order to reduce the weight of the roof and save on costs.

The content the halo-board delivers can be a combination of game action, instant replays, scores, statistics, graphics, animations, advertising and more. In order to drive a 21,517 x 1154 pixel display, a custom control system was developed by Ottawa-based Ross Video and their XPression Tessera engine. The XPression Tessera engine is a real-time graphics designer and controller for large-scale displays. It is an integrated solution of both hardware and software that can provide synchronized playback from multiple real-time engines. It allows users to produce seamless output of video across large and/or irregularly assembled display panels, which is what makes it perfect for sports scoreboards. Resolution can be easily scaled by adding more Tessera nodes.
